Unveiling the Veil

Acrylic on Bamboo, 16.5″ × 23.4″, 2020

Unveiling the Veil

Acrylic on Bamboo, 16.5″ × 23.4″, 2020

Unveiling the Veil began through making. Bamboo mats woven from jute rope, built by hand, then painted with acrylic. Light and colour move across the surface in linear distributions. The shadow cast by the bamboo is not incidental. It becomes another layer, structural rather than painted, shifting with the light.

The material comes from Bareilly, known as Bans Bareilly, a region associated with bamboo in India. Working with it was not a conceptual decision, but a recognition. The surface already held a structure before the image began.

“I am consciously deciding the nature of my experiences, yet unaware of my underlying influences.”

This tension remains within the work. The veil is not removed to reveal something beneath. It is the condition of making itself, where decisions are conscious, but their origins are not fully known.
