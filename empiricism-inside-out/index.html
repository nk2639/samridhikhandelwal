Empiricism (Inside Out)

Wood blocks reflection on wall, Variable size, 2019

"Empiricism (Inside-Out)," elegantly encapsulates the duality of fragmentation and unity. By arranging scattered wood pieces on a mirror to reflect the swastika, you invite viewers to engage with the complexities of cultural symbols and their meanings.

The swastika, a sacred symbol in Hinduism, represents auspiciousness, prosperity, and the cyclical nature of life. Its presence in your piece signifies a shared goal or purpose amidst the chaos of individual experiences. The fragmented wood pieces symbolize the disarray often found in our lives, highlighting how we may feel scattered in our personal journeys. Yet, when viewed through the mirror, these pieces come together to form a cohesive symbol, reminding us that despite our differences and struggles, we are all interconnected and striving towards the same fundamental truths.

This artwork carries a deep impact as it challenges viewers to reflect on their own lives and the collective human experience. It prompts us to consider how, even in our scattered states, we can create meaning and beauty. By using natural materials, you also emphasize a connection to the earth, grounding the swastika's spiritual significance in a tangible reality.

Overall, "Empiricism (Inside-Out)" serves as a poignant reminder of resilience and unity, encouraging introspection about how we can navigate our individual paths while remaining part of a larger whole.
