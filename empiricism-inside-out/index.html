Empiricism (Inside Out)

Fragmentation, reflection, meaning

Installation · Wood blocks, reflection on wall · 2019

Empiricism (Inside Out) was made during a residency at NIV Art Centre, New Delhi, in 2019. Wood blocks are arranged in space, scattered individually, but forming a coherent whole when read together with their reflection on the wall. The physical object and its reflection complete each other. Neither is sufficient alone.

The work begins with a question of fragmentation, how dispersed elements, when brought into relation, produce a form that exceeds any single part.

At the centre is a geometry that carries contradictory histories. In Hindu philosophical and cultural tradition, the swastik is an ancient symbol associated with continuity and the eternal. In the twentieth century, the same form was appropriated and made to carry an opposing weight.

The work does not resolve this contradiction. It holds it. The same form, encountered through different cultural memory, produces different readings. What is seen depends on what is brought into the act of looking.

This is the condition the title names. Knowledge is not contained in the object alone, but formed through the relationship between object and perceiver. Inside out: meaning does not reside in the form, but in the act of looking.
