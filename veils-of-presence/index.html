Veils of Presence

Three shadows, three truths

Installation · Rice paper, charcoal, light, shadow · 2025

Artist statement

Translucent sheets of rice paper are suspended in space, carrying fragments of text and sketches, questions that range from direct to barely visible, and at times withdrawn into absence.

As light shifts, the viewer's shadow enters the work, merging with text and image. The boundary between observer and object collapses. The viewer becomes both reader and participant. The questions no longer remain on paper. They are carried by the body.

The work draws from the philosophical triad of Purusha, the witnessing self; Prakriti, the mutable; and Shunya, emptiness and silence. These ideas are not illustrated but enacted through shifts in visibility, presence, and erasure.

Text appears in multiple states, legible, fading, or absent. Meaning does not settle in a single place. It unfolds through movement, duration, and attention.

A guided voice moves through the space, directing attention without fixing interpretation.

This is not a work to be solved. It is a space to be entered. What is seen remains unstable. What is felt continues beyond the encounter.

Questions from the installation

The installation posed questions at different scales and intensities — some bold declarations, some whispered, some erased into silence. A few of them:

Who are you when no one is watching?
Do you exist beyond your shadow?
What is left when everything else dissolves?
Are you the seer or the seen?
What is restless inside you?
From what fragments is your universe made?
