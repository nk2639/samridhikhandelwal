Reciprocity Failure

Oil and Laser cut on Wood, 16″ × 10″, 2018

Reciprocity Failure

Oil and Laser cut on Wood, 16″ × 10″, 2018

Reciprocity failure is a photography term for the point at which light and shadow stop behaving as expected. Shadows deepen beyond their actual value. The correspondence between source and image breaks down.

This work was made in the United Kingdom, at a distance from home. The surfaces hold elements from the immediate environment, windows, architectural lines, the specific geometries of British light. But the shadows that appear across them do not belong to that space. They are presences without visible origin, carrying the weight of what is elsewhere.

Windows repeat throughout the work. The frame is visible but what lies inside remains inaccessible. Memory operates the same way, the structure is present but the interior does not fully open.

The work does not describe longing. It holds the condition of it, where two worlds occupy the same surface without resolving into one.

What remains unarticulated leaves a trace. The work locates itself within that space.
