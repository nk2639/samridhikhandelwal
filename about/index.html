Samridhi Khandelwal

Contemporary visual artist

Photo by Megan Maloy

Samridhi Khandelwal is a contemporary visual artist from India, based in Jersey City, New Jersey. Her practice explores perception as a shifting condition, shaped by memory, environment, and inner awareness.

Working across oil painting, watercolor, and installation, Khandelwal constructs images and environments that change with light, time, and viewer presence. Her work begins with close observation of everyday moments, reflections, shadows, and architectural fragments, which she reconfigures into visual fields that hold both clarity and ambiguity.

At the core of her practice is an interest in how the internal and external continuously inform one another. Through layering, erasure, and controlled gestures, forms emerge and dissolve, resisting fixed meaning. Surfaces remain open, allowing perception to unfold gradually rather than resolve immediately.

Her visual language is defined by restrained compositions and a muted, earthy palette. These works do not present singular narratives. They create conditions for stillness and attention, where looking becomes a process of introspection.

In her installations, this inquiry extends into space. Using suspended translucent surfaces, light, and shadow, she creates environments that are activated through the viewer’s movement. As viewers navigate the work, their presence becomes embedded within it, dissolving the boundary between observer and observed.

Khandelwal holds a Master of Fine Art in Painting from Coventry University, United Kingdom, and a Bachelor of Fine Arts from Amity University, India. Her work has been exhibited in the United States and internationally. She was recently part of the Project 14C Residency in Jersey City.
