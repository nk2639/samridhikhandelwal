Samridhi Khandelwal

Contemporary visual artist

Photo by Megan Maloy

Samridhi Khandelwal is a contemporary visual artist from India, based in Jersey City, New Jersey, USA. Her practice explores perception as a shifting condition, shaped by light, memory, and inner awareness.

Working across oil painting, watercolour, and installation, Khandelwal builds images and environments where forms emerge and dissolve, resisting fixed meaning. Her work begins with close observation of reflections, draped surfaces, and layered architectural fragments encountered in daily life, which she reconfigures into visual fields that hold both clarity and ambiguity.

Her current practice moves between two ongoing series. The Distance I Carry is a body of watercolours rooted in the experience of migration and perceptual dislocation. Still, Not Still is a series of oil and pastel paintings that extend this inquiry into questions of containment, the feminine form, and what persists beneath the surface.

In her installations, the inquiry extends into space. Using suspended translucent surfaces, light, and shadow, she creates environments activated through the viewer’s movement. As viewers navigate the work, their presence becomes embedded within it, dissolving the boundary between observer and observed.

Khandelwal holds an MA in Painting from Coventry University, United Kingdom, and a BFA from Amity University, India. She received the Finext National Award in 2018. Her work has been exhibited in the United States and India, including at Lalit Kala Akademi, New Delhi, and most recently at Project 14C Residency, Jersey City.
