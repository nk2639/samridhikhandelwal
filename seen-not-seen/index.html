Seen, not Seen

Oil on canvas, 2025–ongoing

Seen, not Seen

Oil on canvas, 2025–ongoing

In Indian sculpture and Renaissance painting alike, the draped figure carries a specific weight, fabric that conceals as much as it reveals, form held within expectation. These paintings begin from that image, not as reference but as structure.

Working in oil, I build surfaces through successive layers, glazing, lifting, reworking. Forms emerge from warm amber and deep umber, close to skin, close to earth. They fold and compress. Edges dissolve. Depth does not resolve. The image holds a tension between containment and release that the paint alone cannot exhaust.

The white lines drawn across in pastel are not outlines. They cross the forms beneath rather than following them, marks that move independently of what has already been built. Neither still nor restless. Present in a different way.

These paintings are connected to an ongoing watercolour practice, The Distance I Carry. Where those works are quiet and provisional, the oils press harder. The inquiry is the same, what is held, what moves through, what remains, but the material demands more. So does the question.

Prakriti, in Indian philosophical thought, is the feminine principle, not passive, but the very force of change and becoming. Still, Not Still does not illustrate that idea. It works from within it.
