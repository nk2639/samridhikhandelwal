The Distance I Carry

Watercolour on paper, 2024–ongoing

The Distance I Carry

Watercolour on paper, 2024–ongoing

The Distance I Carry began after I moved from India to the United States, a transition that introduced a sustained sense of spatial and emotional dislocation. In this condition, my relationship to looking shifted. I found myself observing more than participating, attending to fleeting visual phenomena such as reflections, layered surfaces, and the instability of light.

Working in watercolor, I construct images through successive translucent washes that stain the paper rather than sit on its surface. Forms emerge gradually but resist resolution. They remain provisional, appearing and dissolving within the same field. Fine white lines traverse these surfaces as interruptions, not outlines, functioning as traces that register connection, memory, and the movement between states rather than defining fixed structures.

The compositions do not depict specific sites. Instead, they operate as perceptual fields in which depth is suggested and withdrawn simultaneously. What is visible remains contingent, shaped by shifts in light, proximity, and attention. This instability reflects a mode of perception formed through displacement, where familiarity and distance coexist.

The work engages with the idea of the self as both observer and participant, where perception is not passive but constitutive. In this sense, the paintings align with a framework in which reality is understood as layered and relational, rather than singular or fixed. The distance in these works is not only geographical. It is perceptual, marking the gap between what is seen, what is processed, and what is retained.

These paintings are not intended as aesthetic abstractions. They are structured through lived experience and remain anchored in it. Each work holds a state of transition, where image, memory, and perception remain in continuous negotiation.
