Can You See Me, Can You Feel Me

Installation, 2019

“Can You See Me, Can You Feel Me” explores self-identity and awareness through a question placed directly across the wall. The text does not function as a description, but as a point of address, something that the viewer encounters rather than reads passively.

The lighting casts shadows of those who move through the space, creating an interactive moment where each person encounters their own silhouette. This interaction is not fixed. The shadow shifts, appears, and disappears, producing a moment where the viewer becomes aware of their own presence within the work.

The shadow operates both physically and perceptually. It suggests something beyond the visible body, where what is seen and what is felt do not fully align. The viewer is no longer separate from the work, but briefly becomes part of it.

The work considers how we move through spaces without fully registering ourselves. In this moment, the viewer is confronted with their own image, not as something stable, but as something that is constantly shifting and contingent.

Through this, the installation creates a space where the literal and metaphorical shadow begin to overlap. It brings attention to what appears, what is experienced, and what often remains unacknowledged.
